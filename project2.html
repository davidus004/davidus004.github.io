Data Acquisition Board

Description

The circuits shown form a system that handles the acquisition, transmission, reception, and processing of signals coming from two transducers, such as the AD590 temperature sensor. The sensor converts the physical quantity (temperature) into an electrical signal, in this case a current proportional to the measured temperature. The first block of the system is designed to condition the input signal: it converts the current from the sensor into a proportional voltage. A second block then converts this voltage into a pulse train whose frequency is proportional to the input voltage. This conversion is essential for long-distance transmission, as pulse signals are far less susceptible to electromagnetic interference than continuous analog signals. Once received, the pulse train is further processed. The circuit comprises two identical parallel sections (one per transducer), each of which converts the incoming pulses back into a voltage. This voltage is then filtered to remove any high-frequency noise. Finally, the two conditioned signals are combined, either averaged or difference-amplified, to optimize the system’s sensitivity.
